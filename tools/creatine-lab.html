How To Use This Tool

Fast Workflow

  1. Pick a preset, then change only the inputs that match your real scenario.
  2. Use Mix to estimate dissolved creatine, suspended powder, and water needed.
  3. Use Store only if creatine is premixed before use. Water stored by itself and dry powder do not use the aqueous-loss model.
  4. Use Accumulate for rough body-pool curves. Direct SMM is best, direct FFM is next, and body mass plus body fat is only a proxy.

Useful Scenarios

  • Default bottle: 5 g in 1 L at room temperature.
  • Small glass: 5 g in 250 mL to see why grit can remain at equilibrium.
  • Fridge bottle: cold water stored first, creatine added later, with no premix degradation before adding it.
  • Loading comparison: compare 20 g/day loading with 5 g/day daily in the accumulation tab.

Read Results This Way

  • Dissolved at equilibrium and Suspended powder describe the bottle or glass, not guaranteed absorption.
  • Dissolved loss applies to the dissolved fraction during premixed storage.
  • Modeled saturation, Steady-state dose, and Waste pressure are model outputs, not dosing instructions.
  • Monte Carlo band shows assumption uncertainty. It is not a personal measurement.

Do Not Conclude

  • This does not decide whether creatine is safe for you or recommend a dose.
  • Suspended powder is not automatically wasted if the full suspension is consumed promptly.
  • The brain tCr estimate is a separate reference estimate and does not change with the selected protocol.
  • Body-pool values are rough estimates driven by the inputs and assumptions.
