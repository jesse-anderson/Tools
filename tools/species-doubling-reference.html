Reference Scope / Use At Your Own Risk

This page summarizes literature-reported microbial growth kinetics for comparison and educational use. Real doubling behavior varies with strain lineage, inoculum age, oxygen handling, medium composition, pH control, buffering, milk treatment, and measurement method.

Monoculture reference only. The displayed td values are mono-culture or single-organism benchmarks under the cited conditions. They should not be treated as direct mixed-culture, cofermentation, or symbiotic growth rates.

Primary Yogurt Starters

Fast acidifiers and proteolytic partners used to drive classic yogurt fermentation.

S. thermophilus

Streptococcus thermophilus

Starter

How to Read It

  • td is doubling time during exponential growth, not total fermentation time.
  • μmax is the maximum specific growth rate reported by the paper.
  • When a paper gave multiple temperature or medium conditions, up to three are listed in each card.
  • All listed reference rows should be read as mono-culture benchmarks unless a source explicitly says otherwise.

Formula

Use the standard exponential relationship to convert between growth rate and doubling time.

μ = ln(2) / td td = ln(2) / μ

Media Definitions

  • MRS: de Man, Rogosa, and Sharpe broth, a common lactic-acid-bacteria laboratory medium.
  • MPL: the chemically defined medium used for L. bulgaricus growth testing in Adeghate & Bisschops.
  • SSF: semi-solid fermentation flour blend used in the probiotic kinetics paper; it is not milk.
  • BHI: brain heart infusion broth; "diluted BHI" is a lower-nutrient variant used in some Listeria studies.

Interpretation Notes

  • Starter values favor dairy-facing benchmarks instead of the most aggressive constant-pH maxima.
  • Cofermentation or protocooperation can drive effective doubling times far below the mono-culture values shown here.
  • Do not use this page to infer mixed-culture kinetics without direct literature support for that exact pairing and substrate.
  • SSF rows in the probiotic cards are not milk; they show how much substrate design can change growth.
  • Bifidobacterial values are especially strain- and oxygen-sensitive, so treat them as practical benchmarks.
